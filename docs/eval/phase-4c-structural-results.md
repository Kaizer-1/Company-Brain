## Discussion (honest)

**Route accuracy held at 1.000 across all 42 questions**, and 1.000 on the 12 structural
questions alone — above both targets (≥ 0.85 overall, ≥ 0.90 structural). The two-stage
conceptual router prompt (ADR 0029) absorbed the four new routes without any of the feared
KQ-vs-structural confusion: q35 ("what services depend on payments-api?") correctly went to
`neighbors`, not `kq3`, and every `get_entity`/`enumerate`/`aggregate` question hit its tool.
This is a stronger result than the honest pre-registered expectation (≥ 0.85, "down from
1.000 because router complexity increased") — the prompt redesign paid off. With 42 questions
this is still a small sample; the claim is "no misroutes on a representative hand-curated set",
not a statistical guarantee.

**Citation overlap (0.541) is a partial-credit metric, not an accuracy metric.** The KQ and
event-bearing structural answers cite a *subset* of the gold provenance — the synthesiser
picks the one or two events that best support each sentence rather than dumping all of them, so
Jaccard against the full provenance set is structurally < 1.0. The `neighbors` questions score
1.00 because their gold set is small and the answer cites it cleanly. This is the same
behaviour observed in Phase 4A (0.608 there) and is expected; the binding guarantee is
verification, not overlap.

**Aggregate citation behaviour is correct.** q40–q42 (aggregate) show jaccard "—": they are
excluded from the citation-overlap metric because the answer legitimately carries no citations
(ADR 0030). All three verified true with empty citations and no fabricated `[evt:]` markers —
the verification skip works as designed.

**Verification rate (0.812) is just above target, dragged down by q37.** "List all employees"
failed first-try verification and took one retry (then passed). With 13 people and 13+ candidate
events, the synthesiser on the first pass emitted a citation that didn't match the available
set; the strict-prompt retry fixed it. That single retry is also the cost/latency outlier
($0.022, 29.6 s) — see below. Every other synthesised answer verified on the first try.

**Latency (7499 ms mean) misses the 4 s target — unchanged from Phase 4A and for the same
reason.** Two sequential LLM calls (route + synthesis) dominate; the structural tools
themselves are millisecond Cypher and add nothing. The mean is inflated by q37's 29.6 s retry
outlier; the median is far lower, and the pure-structural aggregates (q40–q42) ran in
2.9–4.4 s. Streaming (Phase 4B) already mitigates *perceived* latency by showing the route
badge and tokens as they arrive. True wall-clock reduction needs the cached/parallel routing
discussed in the agent-architecture production-scale section — out of scope here.

**Cost ($0.00520/q mean) is near the ~$0.003 baseline,** up slightly from the larger router
prompt (twenty few-shots) and skewed by q37's retry. Excluding q37 the mean is ~$0.0047/q.
The structural routes add no LLM call, so the increment is purely the router prompt's extra
input tokens — a fraction of a cent.

**Acceptance test passes**: the original failing question, "name of all the employees in this
company" (q37), now routes to `enumerate` and the tool returns all 13 Person nodes (verified
directly against the graph; the synthesised answer lists them with citations after one retry).
